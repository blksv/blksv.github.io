General Terms and Conditions for Rendering IT Services

Referenced in:
  • Contract Guidelines

Tbilisi, October 25, 2022

This agreement is made by and between [...] (“Client”), and individual entrepreneur Andrei Bolkisev (“Contractor”), individually referred to as “Party” and collectively “Parties”.

In recognition

That the Client, which is a registered business engaged in [...], requires to receive software engineering and development services.

That the Contractor is an established professional providing the following IT services:

  • domain and problem analysis,
  • data modeling,
  • dataflow design,
  • database design,
  • information systems architecture design,
  • software architecture design,
  • API design,
  • mathematical algorithms design and implementation,
  • backend development,
  • fronted development (non-UI),
  • UX design and prototyping,
  • software refactoring, maintenance, and technical support.

That the Parties have discussed and agreed to collaborate on the development of the Client’s software and infrastructure, with the respective roles, rights, and responsibilities as outlined in this arrangement.

Now, therefore, for and in consideration of the premises and the mutual covenants hereinafter entered into, the parties agree as follows:


  1. Term. The contract is effective starting on the date it is signed by both Parties until it is terminated by either Party. The termination of the contract does not free the Parties from the remained obligations unless otherwise specified in the termination agreement.

  2. Services.

    (a) The Contractor shall provide software planning, design, and development services to the Client at the request of the Client which has to provide appropriate specifications as to the scope and content of the requested service.

    (b) The Client shall provide the Contractor with all the the instructions, clarifications, materials, licenses, remote accesses, and other assistance necessary to render the services within one business day of the Contractor’s request.

    (c) The Contractor reserves the right to decline or postpone the Client’s requests at his own discern, except for requests pertaining to the Contractor’s warranty obligations.

    (d) The Contractor reserves the right to cease the rendering of the services as well as warranty obligations in case the Client fails to comply with the terms set herein until the compliance is restored. All the imposed request deadlines extend by the period of cessation.

  3. Changes in specifications.

    (a) The Client may request that reasonable changes be made to the specifications submitted to the Contractor. The Contractor will use its best efforts to implement the requested change at no additional expense to the Client and without delaying the delivery of the originally requested materials.

    (b) If the proposed change requires a delay in the delivery or additional expense to the Client, the Parties shall negotiate the new content, scope, price, and delivery date for the request.

    (c) The Client agrees that the Contractor shall have the sole judgment pertaining to delays or price increases.

    (d) If any difficulties arise in the course of implementing the Client’s request, the Contractor shall immediately notify the Client to negotiate the new content, scope, price, and delivery date for the problematic request.

    (e) The time spent in negotiations is billable regardless of the result.

    (f) All the imposed request deadlines extend by the period of settling the changes.

  4. Compensation.

    (a) The reference hourly rate for the services provided by the Contractor is 50 USD.

    (b) The price for performing requests with well-defined scope and content shall be fixed in advance by the Parties' mutual agreement on a case-by-case basis. Such work shall be paid by the Client when completed by the Contractor.

    (c) When a deadline is imposed on a well-defined request that the Contractor fails to meet, his compensation is reduced by 1% (one percent) for each business day of delay until 20% of the initial price is reached, or the Client has the right to terminate the request without compensation.

    (d) The Client shall pay the Contractor for hours actually spent on performing terminated requests at the reference rate, but no more than the agreed fixed price, regardless of the reason for termination.

    (e) The requests with ill-defined scope (as well as maintenance and bug investigation requests, meetups, engineering and planning sessions, scope-of-work negotiations, and the like) shall be billed hourly at the reference rate (unless mutually agreed otherwise), rounding up to full hours. The Client shall pay for such work of its results.

    (f) The Client may buy from the Contractor a maintenance package, consisting of a limited amount of working hours on ill-defined, maintenance, and minor improvements requests for a discounted rate. The period of validity of the package, the number of working hours, and the discount shall be mutually agreed upon for each package. Upon expiration of the package, the unused hours are not refunded to the Client.

    (g) The Client shall reimburse all the Contractor’s reasonable out-of-pocket expenses pertaining to the Client’s requests.

    (h) The Contractor shall send the Client invoices representing the completed well-defined work, maintenance package fee, license fees, hourly billing for other work performed, and expenses to reimburse. The Client shall pay the invoiced amount within 10 business days to the Contractor’s bank account indicated in the invoice, or notify the Contractor about errors in the invoice or defects in the billed work. Defects resulting from causes that render the Contractor’s warranty inapplicable shall not be the grounds for the Client to reject the billed work.

    (i) By paying the invoice the Client indicates acceptance of the work and services billed.

    (j) The Client shall pay a fine of 1% (one percent) of the total amount due for each business day of delaying the payment. Money transfer fees shall be paid by the Client.

  5. Warranties.

    (a) The Contractor shall investigate and correct errors and deficiencies found by the Client in the submitted work, or caused by the submitted work, at the highest priority and free of charge.

    (b) If the Contractor fails to correct the reported deficiencies in a reasonable time, the Client has the right to reject the submitted work in full or in part, proportionally reducing the compensation payable to the Contractor for the submitted work, or reclaim the appropriate part of the payment already made.

    (c) The warranty does not apply if the reported deficiencies resulted from (a) execution environment specifics, not reported in the original task specification, (b) unresolved inconsistency of requirements, (c) increased hardware requirements to handle more complex processes and/or bigger user base, (d) unresolved interference of the new functionality with the existent one if the desired behavior was not indicated in the original specification, (e) changes in the codebase performed by other people, (f) violation of the rules and protocols regarding the use of the submitted work, (g) alternative interpretation of the original task specification by the Client, (h) any other causes of which the Contractor was not in control or could not reasonably anticipate. In such a case, the Client shall pay for the deficiency investigation at the current Contractor’s hourly rate, which may be different from the stated in this contract. The current Contractor’s rate is shown on the Contractor’s website.

    (d) In no event shall the Contractor be liable for any loss, damage, and lost profits, direct or indirect, arising from the use of the materials submitted to the Client.

    (e) The Client shall indemnify, defend and protect the Contractor from and against all lawsuits and costs of every kind pertaining to the Client's business.

    (f) The Contractor shall indemnify, defend and protect the Client from and against all lawsuits and costs of every kind pertaining to the Developer’s infringement of the intellectual property rights in the materials submitted to the Client, provided these are exploited by the Client as per agreed upon specification.

  6. Intellectual property rights.

    (a) The Contractor shall grant to the Client a type-A license (see definitions below) for any materials submitted, provided that all of the following apply: (a) the materials are developed at a Client’s request, (b) the materials are specific to the business of the Client, (c) the materials do not constitute a business-enabling contribution (i.e. enabling entire Client’s business or a part of it, or give it a significant competitive advantage, with no apparent ways for the Client to achieve the same without the Contractor’s assistance at comparable time/money/other resources costs).

    (b) The Parties shall negotiate and sign an additional property rights transfer or royalty and license agreement for the Client to use any business-enabling contributions made by the Contractor.

    (c) The Contractor shall grant to the Client a type-B license to exploit the materials developed at the Client’s request, but not specific to the business of the Client.

    (d) The Contractor shall grant to the Client a type-C license to exploit the materials developed by the Contractor, but not at the Client’s request.

    (e) The Contractor warrants that any materials submitted to the Client do not infringe any third party intellectual property rights, if exploited by the Client as per agreed upon specification.

  7. License terms.

    (a) The Parties shall agree upon the licensing terms for the materials prepared in fulfillment of the Client’s request before the Contractor starts to work on the request.

    (b) The type of license for submitted materials is indicated in the invoice billing for that materials.

    (c) All of the licenses below are free of charge, irrevocable, and worldwide, unless the invoice indicates otherwise.

    (d) All of the licenses below are effective since the submitted materials are paid in full and until the Client ceases the business pertaining to the materials unless the invoice indicates otherwise. When properly notified, the Contractor shall extend the license to the Client’s successors, or the entities otherwise lawfully received the granted license from the Client, at no costs.

    (e) All of the licenses below shall extend to any affiliates, subsidiaries, and divisions of the Client, and also include the Client’s right to grant sublicenses consistent with the terms of the license. The Client shall be responsible for compliance by each such entity with the terms of the license.

    (f) Type-A license. The Client shall have sole rights to create derivative works, and to use, reproduce, market, distribute, publicly display and perform or otherwise exploit the licensed materials or derivative works. The Contractor shall not grant licenses to use the materials to third parties, but reserves the same rights for his own.

    (g) Type-B license. The Client shall have rights to create derivative works, and to use, reproduce, market, distribute, publicly display and perform or otherwise exploit the licensed materials or derivative works. The Contractor reserves the same rights for his own, and also to grant the same to third parties.

    (h) Type-C license. The Client shall have rights to create derivative works, and to use, reproduce, market, distribute, publicly display and perform or otherwise exploit the licensed materials or derivative works only in the project these were requested for.

  8. Confidentiality. Parties shall not disclose any confidential or proprietary information of either Party, including licenses, designs, processes, program codes, documentation, plans, and reports to the public or any other third party not authorized by both Parties or by the law.

  9. Relationship. The Client understands and agrees that the Contractor is not an employee, agent, or partner of the Client. The Client shall not try in any way to intervene in, control, or monitor the internal processes of the Contractor or his relations with third parties.

  10. Waiver, Modification, or Cancellation. Any waiver, alteration, or modification of any of the terms and provisions set herein shall be in writing and signed by the Parties.

  11. Communication. All notifications and other communications required by this contract shall be in writing. They are regarded as effective when received, as indicated by the services for electronic documents/messages exchange, or after 5 business days after sending by the acknowledged electronic channel. The Parties agree that any communications sent by an acknowledged representative of the Party express the will of such Party. The Parties agree that electronically signed documents shall have the same legal effect as manually signed.

In the event of a controversy or claim arising out of or relating to these terms and conditions, or the breach, validity, or termination of the agreements between the Parties, the Parties shall first negotiate in good faith for thirty days to try to resolve the controversy or claim. If the controversy or claim is unresolved after these negotiations, the matter shall be settled in the appropriate court at the location of the respondent.

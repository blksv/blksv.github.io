A clever guy will think about your problems. 1$/min.

I'm a software engineer with 16+ years of professional (and ~25 years overall) experience in a wild range of projects, from microcontroller programming and scientific research to nationwide information systems, see CV.

I do full-cycle almost-full-stack engineering and development: problem analysis, data flow design, decomposition into services, api design, database design, mathematical algorithms design and implementation, backend development, frontend development (non-user-facing parts), UX prototyping, maintenance, and technical support. I don't do systems administration of any kind (network, database, security, and so on), docker-compose.yml is my max.

As I prefer to work as a contractor, my typical mode of operation is to bring about some large block of functionality (often business-enabling) and then support and evolve it, sometimes making additional large contributions to the project.

My defaults are Python/fastapi for web-backend, Postgres as DB, C++ (migrating to Ada2022) or Java for cpu-intensive backend services, JS/React(preact) for frontend, ReactNative for mobile. My experience allows me to easily adopt new stacks when this results in more elegant and manageable solutions.

For well-defined problems, I usually set a fixed price, while technical support, research, and similar work are billed per hour at 50$/hr rate (as of now), with 1-hour granularity (I offer discount packages on that). Bugfixing is free of charge (as a part of my contractual obligations to provide quality software), as well as the license to use my pre-developed solutions. Details are in Contract Guidelines.

I always try to find the most elegant, small, and efficient solution to maximize project velocity and minimize development, maintenance, and infrastructure costs. I'm proud to have some quite sophisticated projects launched in just several months with a team of literally two-and-a-half developers (or even less), running on very modest hardware, and then successfully picked by the next generations of developers almost without assistance from the original team. But this results from a specific style, which may not suit you.

Special abilities

  1. Making simple things simple. I can spend a lot of time trying to find the most simple solution to a problem. A day of thinking sometimes saves weeks of coding and then years of maintaining.
  2. Quick pick up of new domains and tech stacks. As long as it fits into the "making simple things simple" paradigm.
  3. Whole-picture full-cycle systems thinking. I care about your infrastructure and maintenance costs, project velocity, integrations with adjacent systems, network timings and user experience. Nope, you can't make me "mind my own business".
  4. Managing technical debt. Quick-and-dirty solutions which are compact and local. Simplicity and tractability above efficiency in non-critical paths. Incremental refactoring where previous solutions hinder velocity/performance.
